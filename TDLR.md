# "Today I learned speech"
![This is an introduction photo](img/I_LIKE_POETRY.png)

### The Opening
"I’ve been in business for almost two years now. But honestly? It wasn’t until last week that I felt like I actually finished my 'first season' in the big leagues.

I just wrapped up my first full financial year. The taxes are filed, the payouts are processed, and the dust has finally settled. For the first time, the abstract idea of 'being an entrepreneur' became a very concrete reality of spreadsheets and legal obligations."

### The Internal Conflict
"What they don’t tell you when you start a company is the weird mental gymnastics you have to do every day. You’re constantly switching hats. One minute, you’re the employee, grinding out the work and staying in the weeds. The next, you have to be the manager, looking at the big picture and making the hard calls.

I’ll be the first to admit: I don’t know everything. In fact, I still feel pretty dumb at least once a day. I’ve spent the last year on the phone with my lawyer and my accountant, asking questions that—in my head—felt like things I should have known months ago. I’ve asked the same questions over and over again because I just wasn't sure. I was terrified of making that one 'rookie mistake' that brings the whole house down."

### The Turning Point
"But through that back-and-forth, and through that occasional feeling of being 'the slowest person in the room,' I learned the single most important lesson of these last two years:

If you don’t know, you have to ask.

We have this toxic idea in business that a founder needs to be the person with all the answers. We think our 'integrity' or our 'authority' is tied to our expertise. But let me tell you something: If your sense of integrity is threatened by acknowledging that you don’t know something, you need to get rid of that ego immediately. It is dead weight. It’s a liability."

### The Call to Action
"If you don't know it, you need to ask. You need to ask it clearly, and sometimes, you need to ask it loudly to move forward. Because here is the cold, hard truth: If you don’t ask, nobody else is going to do it for you. Nobody is coming to save your business from your own silence.

And the funniest part? The thing that kept me awake at night? It turns out that nobody cares. Your accountant doesn't care if you're confused. Your lawyer doesn't care if you've never heard a specific term before. To ask is normal. To learn is the job.

So, stop trying to look like you have it all figured out. Start asking the 'dumb' questions, get the information you need, and keep moving. That's how you actually win the season."
